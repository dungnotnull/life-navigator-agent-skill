# Scam Detection Patterns

## Why This Matters

Many non-tech users — especially seniors — receive fraudulent letters, calls, and
emails designed to look like official government or collection notices.
Always check for scam signals before processing a document.

---

## RED FLAGS — Almost Certainly a Scam

| Signal | Why It's a Scam | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Demands gift cards as payment | No legitimate agency accepts gift cards | "Send $500 in Google Play cards to settle your IRS debt" |
| Requests wire transfer or cryptocurrency | Untraceable — government never asks for this | "Wire $2,000 to this account within 24 hours" |
| Threatens arrest if you don't pay immediately | IRS and SSA don't arrest people for unpaid taxes/benefits without court process | "You will be arrested in 2 hours if you don't call" |
| Threatens to send police/FBI immediately | Real law enforcement doesn't call to warn you | "The police are on their way unless you pay now" |
| Caller ID shows "IRS" or "SSA" but it feels wrong | Caller ID can be spoofed easily | Official agencies send letters first, almost never call |
| Asks for full Social Security Number by phone/email | Real agencies already have it — they ask for last 4 to verify | "Give me your full SSN to verify your account" |
| Urgent language + scary consequences + no time to think | Designed to create panic | "Your account will be seized in 1 hour" |
| No specific account number or your name | Real correspondence is always personalized | Generic letter with no name or account number |
| Poor spelling or grammar throughout | Official government letters are professionally written | "The IRS has notice that you owes $3,400 in backtatxes" |
| P.O. Box as the only return address (for official agencies) | Real IRS, SSA, CMS use specific addresses | Return: P.O. Box 99999, Phoenix AZ |
| "Medicare" selling something / calling to "update your card" | Medicare doesn't call to sell or update cards | "We need your Medicare number to send your new card" |
| Threatens your Medicare/Social Security will be "suspended" | These can only be changed through official processes with notice | "Your Social Security number has been suspended" |

---

## YELLOW FLAGS — Could Be Legitimate, Verify First

| Signal | What to Do |
|---|---|
| Collection letter for a debt you don't recognize | Send written debt validation request within 30 days |
| IRS letter you weren't expecting | Call IRS directly at 1-800-829-1040 to verify |
| Court summons that seems unusual | Call the court clerk directly (number from official court website, not the letter) |
| Official-looking letter asking you to call a 1-900 number | 1-900 numbers charge you — never call for official business |
| Letter asking you to "claim your prize" and pay fees | Classic advance fee fraud — legitimate prizes don't require upfront payment |
| Social Security "overpayment" notice by phone only | Real SSA overpayment notices come by mail; verify at ssa.gov or 1-800-772-1213 |
| Email from "Medicare" | Medicare communicates by mail, not email |

## How to Verify a Suspicious Document

### For IRS:
- Call IRS directly: **1-800-829-1040**
- Check your IRS account: **irs.gov** (create an account to see all notices)
- Real IRS notices always have a notice number in the upper right corner
- Real IRS notices list a phone number that you can verify at irs.gov/notice

### For Social Security (SSA):
- Call SSA: **1-800-772-1213**
- Visit your local SSA office
- Create a "my Social Security" account at ssa.gov

### For Medicare:
- Call Medicare: **1-800-MEDICARE** (1-800-633-4227)
- Real Medicare card is red, white, and blue
- Medicare will never call you to sell anything

### For debt collectors:
- Ask for written validation of the debt (you have 30 days from first contact)
- Look up the collection agency independently — don't use the number in the letter
- Check the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau database

### For courts:
- Call the court clerk directly — number from the **official court website**, not the letter
- Verify the case number on the official court website (most courts have online case lookup)

## Script: How to Tell the User About a Scam

When a document appears to be a scam:

```
⚠️ THIS LOOKS LIKE IT MIGHT BE A SCAM

Based on what you've shared, this document has several warning signs
of fraud. Here's what I noticed:

• [Specific red flag 1]
• [Specific red flag 2]

What to do:
1. Do NOT call any number in this letter
2. Do NOT send any money, gift cards, or personal information
3. [Verify through official channel] instead
4. You can report this scam to [FTC at reportfraud.ftc.gov]

If you're still unsure, call [official agency number] directly to ask
if they sent you anything.
```

## Common Scam Types Targeting Non-Tech Users

### Medicare Scams
- Fake Medicare card replacement calls
- "Free" medical equipment offers to get your Medicare number
- Billing fraud using your Medicare number

### IRS/Tax Scams
- "You owe back taxes — pay now or be arrested" calls
- Fake IRS letters with misspelled words
- "Tax refund" emails asking you to click a link

### Social Security Scams
- "Your SSN has been suspended" robo-calls
- "You'll lose your benefits if you don't call" voicemails

### Debt Collection Scams
- Fake "payday loan" debts you never took
- Phantom debt collection (debt past statute of limitations)
- "We're suing you today" calls

### Lottery / Prize Scams
- "You've won — pay a fee to collect"
- Foreign lottery notifications

### Senior-Specific Red Flags to Always Mention
- If user is 65+ and received an unexpected call: extremely high fraud risk
- If user mentions "they said don't tell your family": this is a classic manipulation tactic
- If user says "they seemed very official": scammers are trained to sound official
