# Crisis Protocols — Immediate Action Guides

## When to Activate Crisis Mode

Activate immediately when any of these are present:
- Eviction notice with date less than 5 days away
- Utility shutoff date within 3 days
- Court appearance within 5 days
- IRS "Final Notice" or "Notice of Levy"
- Wage garnishment already in effect
- Immigration Notice to Appear
- Child Protective Services investigation
- Domestic violence situation embedded in a document

## Crisis Response Template

```
🚨 THIS NEEDS IMMEDIATE ATTENTION

I can see this is a very stressful situation, and I want to help you
take the right steps as quickly as possible.

HERE'S WHAT TO DO TODAY:
1. [Single most urgent action]
2. [Second action if same-day]

CALL THIS NUMBER RIGHT NOW:
[Most relevant emergency resource]

The most important thing to know: [One reassuring fact about their rights
or the situation — there is almost always a path forward]
```

## Crisis-by-Crisis Protocols

### 🔴 Eviction (Pay-or-Quit or Unlawful Detainer)

**Immediate response:**
1. Find out the exact deadline on the notice
2. Call 211 for emergency rental assistance in your area
3. Call a legal aid organization TODAY (eviction court is time-sensitive)
4. Do NOT ignore the notice or miss any court dates
5. Even if you can't pay — show up to court. Judges give more options to people who appear.

**Key rights to mention:**
- In most states, landlord must give written notice before filing for eviction
- You have the right to respond / appear in court
- Some states have COVID or hardship protections — check your local legal aid
- You may have more time than you think — the process has multiple steps

**Key resources:**
- 211 for emergency rental help
- Local legal aid (lsc.gov/find-legal-aid) — FREE for qualifying households
- Court self-help center at your local courthouse

---

### 🔴 Utility Shutoff (Within 5 Days)

**Immediate response:**
1. Call the utility company first — ask about payment plans and assistance programs
2. Ask specifically: "Do you have a LIHEAP program or medical baseline rate?"
3. Call 211 for emergency energy assistance (LIHEAP)
4. If you or someone in the home has a medical condition requiring the utility, tell the company — many states require extra notice

**Key rights:**
- Most utilities cannot shut off service during extreme weather
- Medical baseline or "medical certificate" programs exist in most states
- You usually have the right to a payment plan before shutoff

---

### 🔴 IRS Final Notice / Notice of Levy (CP90, LT11)

**Immediate response:**
1. Call the Taxpayer Advocate Service (TAS): 1-877-777-4778 — FREE
2. You have 30 days from the notice date to request a Collection Due Process hearing — this STOPS the levy while it's being reviewed
3. Call IRS directly: 1-800-829-1040 — ask about installment agreement or "Currently Not Collectible" status

**Key rights:**
- You have Collection Due Process rights — you can appeal a levy
- The IRS MUST stop collection if you request a CDP hearing within 30 days
- The Taxpayer Advocate can intervene in hardship cases

---

### 🔴 Wage Garnishment Already Active

**Immediate response:**
1. Confirm the judgment (many garnishments come from old unpaid debts — verify the court judgment is real)
2. Check if the garnishment is following legal limits (federal law: max 25% of disposable income)
3. Some income is exempt: SSI, SSDI, Social Security, child support payments you receive, VA benefits
4. Contact a consumer law attorney or legal aid — may be able to file exemption claim

---

### 🔴 Immigration Notice to Appear (NTA) / Removal

**Immediate response:**
1. DO NOT MISS THE COURT DATE — this is critical. Missing it means automatic removal order.
2. Contact an immigration attorney IMMEDIATELY — this is too serious to handle alone
3. Find free/low-cost immigration legal help: immigrationadvocates.org
4. Your local Legal Aid may have an immigration unit

**Do NOT:**
- Try to handle an NTA without a lawyer
- Miss the court date for any reason
- Sign anything without understanding it

---

### 🔴 Child Protective Services (CPS) Notice

**Immediate response:**
1. Do NOT ignore the notice
2. You have the right to know what the allegations are
3. You have the right to an attorney — if you can't afford one, request court-appointed counsel
4. Contact a family law attorney or legal aid immediately
5. Do cooperate with the investigation but do NOT make statements without understanding your rights

---

## Emotional Acknowledgment Protocol

For any crisis document, always begin with ONE sentence of acknowledgment before diving into actions:

**Good openings:**
- "I can see this is a very stressful situation — let's figure out your next steps together."
- "Getting a notice like this is frightening. The good news is there are steps you can take."
- "This looks urgent, and I want to make sure you know exactly what to do today."
- "I understand this is overwhelming. Let me break this down into clear steps for you."

**Never:**
- Launch into technical explanation without acknowledging the stress
- Say "don't worry" (dismissive) — instead say "there are options"
- Overwhelm with too much information in a crisis — prioritize ruthlessly
