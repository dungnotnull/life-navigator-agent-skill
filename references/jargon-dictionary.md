# Jargon Dictionary — 200+ Plain English Translations

## How to Use

When decoding any document, replace every jargon term with its plain English
version from this dictionary. After the first use of a technical term, show:
**[Technical term]** (plain English explanation in parentheses).

## 🏥 Medical & Insurance Jargon

| Term | Plain English |
|---|---|
| Explanation of Benefits (EOB) | A statement from your insurance company showing what they paid and what you owe — **it is NOT a bill** |
| Deductible | The amount you must pay out of pocket each year before insurance starts covering costs |
| Out-of-pocket maximum | The most you'll ever pay in one year — after this, insurance covers 100% |
| Premium | The monthly fee you pay to have insurance |
| Copay | A fixed amount you pay for a visit or prescription (like $20 at a doctor visit) |
| Coinsurance | The percentage you pay after your deductible (like 20% of a bill) |
| In-network | A doctor or hospital that has a contract with your insurance — usually cheaper |
| Out-of-network | A doctor or hospital without a contract — usually more expensive |
| Prior authorization (PA) | When your insurance requires permission before covering a test or treatment |
| Formulary | The list of drugs your insurance will cover |
| Step therapy | Requiring you to try cheaper drugs before they'll cover a more expensive one |
| Allowed amount | The maximum amount insurance will pay for a service |
| Balance billing | When a provider charges you the difference between their rate and what insurance paid |
| Coordination of benefits | When you have two insurance plans, they work together to figure out who pays what |
| Subrogation | Insurance company's right to recover money they paid if someone else was at fault |
| Medicare Part A | Hospital insurance (stays, skilled nursing, hospice) |
| Medicare Part B | Medical insurance (doctor visits, outpatient services, preventive care) |
| Medicare Part C (Medicare Advantage) | Private insurance plan that replaces original Medicare |
| Medicare Part D | Prescription drug coverage |
| Medigap / Supplement | Extra private insurance to cover what Medicare doesn't pay |
| IRMAA | A higher Medicare premium charged to people with higher incomes |
| MSN (Medicare Summary Notice) | Medicare's version of an EOB — shows what Medicare paid |
| ABN (Advance Beneficiary Notice) | A form Medicare patients sign when insurance might not cover a service |
| LIS / Extra Help | Federal program to help low-income Medicare members pay for drugs |
| SHIP | State Health Insurance Assistance Program — free counseling for Medicare |
| CDR (Continuing Disability Review) | SSA checking if you're still disabled |
| EOB | See Explanation of Benefits |
| ICD-10 code | A medical diagnosis code (like Z23 for vaccination visit) |
| CPT code | A medical procedure code (what your doctor did) |
| NPI | A doctor's ID number |
| Patient responsibility | The part of the bill YOU owe after insurance |
| Adjustment | An amount taken off your bill |
| Write-off | Amount the provider agreed not to charge (discount) |
| Collection / Sent to collections | Your debt has been sold to a collection company |
| Itemized bill | A detailed list of every charge — you have the right to request this |
| Surprise billing / Balance billing | Being billed by an out-of-network doctor you didn't choose |

## 🏠 Housing & Lease Jargon

| Term | Plain English |
|---|---|
| Lease / Rental Agreement | The contract between you and your landlord |
| Tenant | The person renting (you) |
| Landlord / Lessor | The property owner |
| Security deposit | Money held by landlord during your stay, returned when you leave (if no damage) |
| Month-to-month tenancy | Renting without a fixed end date — either party can end with proper notice |
| Fixed-term lease | A lease with a specific end date (like 12 months) |
| Holdover tenant | A tenant who stays past the lease end date |
| Notice to Vacate | A legal notice requiring you to leave |
| Pay or Quit Notice | A notice saying: pay what you owe within X days or leave |
| Unlawful Detainer | The formal court case a landlord files to evict you |
| Eviction | The legal process to remove a tenant |
| Writ of Possession | A court order giving the landlord the right to take back the property |
| Normal wear and tear | Minor damage that's expected (like small nail holes) — landlord can't deduct this |
| Habitability | The property must be safe and livable — this is the landlord's legal duty |
| Right of entry | Rules about when and how the landlord can enter your home |
| Subletting / Sublease | Renting your apartment to someone else (usually needs landlord approval) |
| Joint and several liability | If you have roommates, each person is responsible for the entire rent |
| Late fee | A fee charged when rent is paid after the due date |
| Grace period | A few days after rent is due before late fees apply |
| Escrow | A neutral account that holds money |
| Escrow impound | Your mortgage payment includes extra money for taxes and insurance |
| PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) | Insurance you pay if your down payment was less than 20% |
| HOA (Homeowners Association) | An organization that manages a condo or neighborhood and collects fees |
| Special assessment | A one-time extra fee from your HOA for a major expense |
| CC&Rs | Rules and restrictions from your HOA |
| Notice of Default | First step in foreclosure — you've missed mortgage payments |
| Foreclosure | Legal process where the bank takes your home because you stopped paying |
| Short sale | Selling your home for less than you owe (with lender's permission) |
| Section 8 / HCV | A federal rental assistance program for low-income households |
| HAP (Housing Assistance Payment) | The portion of your rent paid by the government in Section 8 |
| Utility allowance | An amount deducted from Section 8 rent if you pay utilities |

## 💰 Financial & Debt Jargon

| Term | Plain English |
|---|---|
| Creditor | A person or company you owe money to |
| Debtor | You — the person who owes money |
| Principal | The original amount borrowed (not including interest) |
| Interest | The fee charged for borrowing money |
| APR (Annual Percentage Rate) | The yearly interest rate on a loan or credit card |
| Minimum payment | The smallest amount you must pay each month to avoid a late fee |
| Grace period | Time after your due date before interest is charged on new purchases |
| Collection agency | A company that buys or manages unpaid debts |
| FDCPA | The law that protects you from abusive debt collectors |
| Validation of debt | Your right to ask a collector to prove the debt is real |
| Statute of limitations | A time limit on how long a creditor can sue you for a debt |
| Judgment | A court order saying you owe money — allows wage garnishment or bank levy |
| Wage garnishment | When money is taken directly from your paycheck to pay a debt |
| Bank levy | When money is taken directly from your bank account to pay a debt |
| Lien | A legal claim on your property because of an unpaid debt |
| Garnishee | Your employer or bank that is ordered to take money from you |
| Chapter 7 bankruptcy | A type of bankruptcy that wipes out most debts ("liquidation") |
| Chapter 13 bankruptcy | A type of bankruptcy with a repayment plan over 3-5 years |
| Discharge | When a debt is legally eliminated by bankruptcy |
| Exempt assets | Things bankruptcy can't take (often your home, car up to a value, retirement accounts) |
| IRS CP14 | Notice that you owe taxes — first notice |
| IRS CP2000 | IRS proposing a change to your return based on income they received info about |
| IRS CP503 | Second reminder about balance due |
| IRS CP504 | Intent to levy your state tax refund — urgent |
| IRS CP90 / LT11 | Final notice before levy — URGENT |
| Tax levy | IRS taking your wages, bank account, or property for unpaid taxes |
| Offer in Compromise | An agreement with IRS to pay less than the full amount owed |
| Installment Agreement | A payment plan with the IRS |
| Currently Not Collectible | IRS status if you truly can't pay — they pause collection |
| FAFSA EFC / SAI | The number used to calculate how much financial aid you qualify for |
| Subsidized loan | A student loan where the government pays interest while you're in school |
| Unsubsidized loan | A student loan where interest builds from the first day |
| PSLF | Public Service Loan Forgiveness — loan forgiveness for nonprofit/government workers |
| IDR (Income-Driven Repayment) | Student loan payment plan based on your income |
| Default (student loan) | Missing 270+ days of payments — serious consequences |
| Rehabilitation | A way to get out of student loan default by making 9 payments |
| Consolidation | Combining multiple student loans into one |
| Credit utilization | How much of your available credit you're using — affects credit score |
| Hard inquiry | When a lender checks your credit (can lower your score slightly) |
| FCRA | The law protecting your rights about your credit report |

## 🏛️ Government & Legal Jargon

| Term | Plain English |
|---|---|
| Plaintiff | The person suing |
| Defendant | The person being sued |
| Summons | An official notice that a lawsuit has been filed and you must respond |
| Complaint | The document explaining what someone is suing you for |
| Default judgment | If you don't respond to a lawsuit, the court automatically rules against you |
| Answer (to a summons) | Your formal written response to a lawsuit |
| Statute | A law passed by a legislature |
| Regulation | A rule made by a government agency to implement a law |
| Due process | Your right to fair treatment and a chance to be heard before action is taken |
| Appeal | Asking a higher authority to review a decision you disagree with |
| Administrative hearing | A formal meeting to appeal a government decision |
| ALJ (Administrative Law Judge) | A judge for government benefit hearings (like Social Security) |
| Notice of Appeal | A form you file to start the appeal process |
| Brief | A written argument in a legal case |
| Affidavit | A written statement sworn to be true |
| Notarize | To have a notary public witness and certify a signature |
| Power of Attorney (POA) | A document letting someone legally act for you |
| Healthcare proxy | A document naming someone to make medical decisions if you can't |
| Conservatorship / Guardianship | A court arrangement where someone manages another person's affairs |
| FICA | Social Security and Medicare taxes taken from your paycheck |
| SSA | Social Security Administration — manages SS, SSI, and SSDI |
| SSI (Supplemental Security Income) | Benefits for low-income disabled or elderly people |
| SSDI (Social Security Disability Insurance) | Benefits for disabled workers who paid into Social Security |
| COLA | Cost of Living Adjustment — annual increase to Social Security benefits |
| SNAP | Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program — food stamps |
| EBT | Electronic Benefit Transfer — the card used for SNAP benefits |
| WIC | Women, Infants, and Children — food program for young families |
| TANF | Temporary Assistance for Needy Families — cash assistance |
| USCIS | U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services |
| I-94 | Arrival/departure record showing how long you can stay in the US |
| RFE (Request for Evidence) | USCIS asking for more documents about your immigration case |
| NOA (Notice of Action) | USCIS notice confirming they received your application |
| NTA (Notice to Appear) | A document ordering someone to appear in immigration court |
| Green card | Permanent resident card — legal permanent residence in the US |
| Naturalization | The process of becoming a US citizen |
| Adjustment of status | Applying for a green card while already in the US |

## 📋 Employment Jargon

| Term | Plain English |
|---|---|
| At-will employment | Either you or your employer can end employment at any time (most US states) |
| Non-compete | An agreement not to work for competitors for a period after leaving |
| Non-solicitation | An agreement not to recruit coworkers or clients after leaving |
| NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) | Agreement to keep company information confidential |
| Vesting | The schedule for when employer contributions to your retirement account become yours |
| 401(k) match | Free money your employer adds to your retirement account based on your contribution |
| HSA (Health Savings Account) | A tax-free account to save money for medical expenses |
| FSA (Flexible Spending Account) | Similar to HSA but "use it or lose it" each year |
| COBRA | A law letting you keep your workplace health insurance after leaving a job |
| COBRA election period | 60 days to decide whether to continue coverage under COBRA |
| Severance | Money paid when you're laid off — not required by law in most cases |
| WARN Act | Requires employers to give 60 days notice before mass layoffs |
| Workers' compensation | Insurance that pays if you're injured on the job |
| IME (Independent Medical Exam) | A medical exam ordered by an insurance company (not your own doctor) |
| FMLA | Family and Medical Leave Act — up to 12 weeks of unpaid protected leave |
| W-2 | Annual tax form showing what your employer paid you and withheld |
| 1099 | Tax form for freelance or contract income |
