# Phone Script Templates — What to Say When You Call

## How to Use These Scripts

These are word-for-word scripts. The user can read directly from the screen
or print them out. Always customize with the actual names, numbers, and dates
from the document before presenting.

---

## SCRIPT-01: Calling About a Medical Bill

```
📞 When to use: Disputing, understanding, or setting up a payment plan for a hospital/doctor bill

"Hello, my name is [YOUR FULL NAME].
I'm calling about a bill I received for [service or date].
My account number is [number from the bill].

I'd like to [choose one]:
  → understand the charges on this bill
  → find out if I qualify for financial assistance
  → set up a payment plan
  → dispute a charge I don't think is correct

Can you help me with that?"

---
Things to have ready:
✓ The bill in front of you
✓ Your insurance card (if you have insurance)
✓ The account number from the top of the bill
✓ A pen and paper

Questions you can ask:
→ "Can you send me an itemized bill?" (list of every individual charge)
→ "Do you have a charity care or financial assistance program?"
→ "What is the minimum I need to pay to avoid collections?"
→ "Will you accept a lower amount as payment in full?"
→ "Can I set up a payment plan with no interest?"

Write down:
→ The name of the person you spoke with
→ The date and time of the call
→ Any reference number they give you
→ What they agreed to do
```

## SCRIPT-02: Calling Insurance About a Denial

```
📞 When to use: Your claim was denied or not fully paid

"Hello, my name is [YOUR FULL NAME].
I'm calling about a claim that was denied.
My member ID is [number from your insurance card].
The claim is for [service] on [date].
The EOB or denial letter reference number is [number].

I'd like to understand why this was denied and
how I can appeal this decision.

Can you help me with that?"

---
Questions to ask:
→ "What is the specific reason code for this denial?"
→ "What does my plan say about coverage for this service?"
→ "What is the process to file an appeal?"
→ "What is the deadline for filing an appeal?"
→ "Can my doctor submit additional information to support the claim?"
→ "Is there an expedited appeal process if this is urgent?"
→ "Can I request an External Review?"

Write down:
→ The denial reason code and explanation
→ The appeal filing deadline
→ The appeal address or fax number
→ The name of the representative and the call reference number
```

## SCRIPT-03: Calling Medicare

```
📞 Medicare: 1-800-MEDICARE (1-800-633-4227)
   Available 24/7

"Hello, my name is [YOUR FULL NAME].
My Medicare number is [the number on your red, white, and blue card].
My date of birth is [date].

I'm calling because I received a Medicare Summary Notice (MSN)
dated [date] and I have a question about [specific item].

Can you help me understand [specific question]?"

---
Common reasons to call Medicare:
→ A charge on your MSN you don't recognize
→ You think your Medicare Advantage plan made a mistake
→ You want to know if a service is covered before you get it
→ You received a call or letter that seems suspicious
→ You want to report Medicare fraud

Note: Medicare will NEVER call you asking for your Medicare number or
to sell you something. If you get such a call, hang up.
```

## SCRIPT-04: Calling Social Security (SSA)

```
📞 SSA: 1-800-772-1213
   Monday–Friday, 8 AM – 7 PM local time
   (Wait times are shorter early morning and mid-week)

"Hello, my name is [YOUR FULL NAME].
My Social Security number is [your SSN — last 4 digits may be sufficient].
My date of birth is [date].

I'm calling about a letter I received dated [date].
The reference number on the letter is [number if shown].

I'd like to [choose]:
  → understand what this letter means
  → ask about an overpayment notice
  → request a waiver for an overpayment
  → get information about my benefits
  → report a change in my situation

Can you help me with that?"

---
Write down:
→ The name of the representative
→ Their direct extension if possible
→ Any case or ticket number
→ Exactly what they said they will do and the timeline
```

## SCRIPT-05: Calling the IRS

```
📞 IRS Individual Assistance: 1-800-829-1040
   Monday–Friday, 7 AM – 7 PM local time
   (Wait times are VERY long — call right when they open)

"Hello, my name is [YOUR FULL NAME].
My Social Security number is [SSN].
I'm calling about a notice I received.
The notice number is [CP number — found in upper right corner].
The notice is dated [date].

I'd like to [choose]:
  → understand what this notice means
  → set up a payment plan (installment agreement)
  → ask about an offer in compromise
  → dispute a proposed change on my return
  → find out if my return has been processed

Can you help me with that?"

---
Important tips:
→ Have a copy of the relevant tax return in front of you
→ Have the notice in front of you
→ Be prepared to wait on hold
→ Ask for a "case number" for your call so you can reference it later

If you're in serious hardship:
→ Ask to speak with a "Taxpayer Advocate" or call TAS directly: 1-877-777-4778
```

## SCRIPT-06: Calling a Debt Collector

```
📞 When to use: You received a collection letter and want to verify the debt

"Hello, my name is [YOUR FULL NAME].
I received a letter from your company dated [date].
The reference number on the letter is [number].

I am exercising my right under the FDCPA to request written
verification of this debt, including:
- The name of the original creditor
- The amount owed and how it was calculated
- Proof that your company has the right to collect this debt

Please send this information to me in writing at:
[YOUR ADDRESS]

I am not acknowledging that this debt is mine or that it is valid
until I receive this verification.

Thank you."

---
Important: You have 30 days from the date you first received their letter
to send a written debt validation request. This PAUSES collection activity
while they verify. Send your written request by certified mail with return receipt.

Do NOT:
→ Give your bank account or credit card number
→ Promise to pay anything until you've verified the debt
→ Ignore the letter — that can lead to a default judgment
```

## SCRIPT-07: Calling Your Landlord or Property Manager

```
📞 When to use: About a rent notice, repair request, or dispute

"Hello, my name is [YOUR FULL NAME].
I live at [your address], unit [number].
I'm calling about [choose]:
  → a notice I received dated [date]
  → a repair issue that needs to be addressed
  → my security deposit

[State your specific question or concern clearly]

I'd like to resolve this as quickly as possible.
Can you help me with that?"

---
Tips:
→ Follow up every phone call with a text or email summarizing what was said
   ("Just following up on our call today — you confirmed that [X]")
→ Keep records of all communication
→ Know your state's landlord-tenant laws (search "[your state] landlord tenant rights")
```

## SCRIPT-08: Calling 211

```
📞 Dial 2-1-1 from any phone (free call)
   Available in most US areas, 24/7

"Hello, I'm looking for help with [describe your situation briefly]:
  → emergency rent/utility assistance
  → food assistance
  → housing resources
  → mental health services
  → other local services

I'm located in [your city and zip code].

Can you tell me what programs are available in my area?"

---
Note: 211 connects you to trained specialists who know local resources.
They can help with almost any basic needs situation.
The call is free and confidential.
```
