# 🧭 Life Navigator Agent

> **Decodes confusing official documents into plain English
> — then tells you exactly what to do next.**

Built for seniors, caregivers, new immigrants, first-time renters, patients,
and anyone who receives an official letter or notice and doesn't know what to do.

I can see this is a very stressful situation. Let's focus

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT THIS SAYS (IN PLAIN ENGLISH)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Your landlord is saying you owe $1,450 in rent

• If you don't do one of those things, they can file
  for eviction (a court case to remove you)

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SHOULD YOU BE WORRIED?
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Yes — this needs your attention very soon. But there ARE
steps you can take, and help is available. Don't panic —
take it one step at a time.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT TO DO NEXT
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Call 2-1-1 right now
   → Dial 2-1-1 from any phone. Ask about emergency
     rental assistance in your area. There may be
     funds available to help pay your rent today.

2. Contact a legal aid organization today
   → They provide FREE legal help for eviction cases.
     Find yours at lsc.gov/find-legal-aid

3. Try to contact your landlord
   → If you can pay, do so and get written confirmation.
     If you can't pay all of it, ask if they'll accept
     a payment plan.

⚠️ IMPORTANT: Count the days carefully from the date
on this notice. Missing the deadline leads to a court
case being filed against you.

🆘 WHERE TO GET MORE HELP
• 211 (dial 2-1-1) — emergency rental assistance
• Legal Aid: lsc.gov/find-legal-aid
• HUD Housing Counselors: 1-800-569-4287

## Special Handling for Different Users

### 👴 Senior Mode
- Even simpler language
- Extra scam warnings (seniors are targeted 3x more)
- Free resources: SHIP, SMP, Eldercare Locator, BenefitsCheckUp
- Option to write a letter instead of calling

### 🌍 New Immigrant Mode
- Ultra-short sentence structure
- Explanation of US-specific systems (credit scores, SSNs)
- Free immigration legal help resources
- Strong recommendation for DOJ-accredited representatives

### 💊 Caregiver Mode
- HIPAA and Power of Attorney considerations
- Resources for the caregiver, not just the patient
- Acknowledgment of caregiving stress

### 🚨 Crisis Mode
- Emotional acknowledgment first
- Emergency resources immediately
- Compressed critical path — just what to do TODAY

## Scam Protection Built In

Every document is automatically checked for fraud signals before analysis:

| Red Flag | What It Means |
|---|---|
| "Pay in gift cards" | Scam — no government agency accepts gift cards |
| "You'll be arrested today" | Scam — government sends letters first |
| Grammar errors throughout | Likely fake document |
| Wire transfer demand | Scam — untraceable payment |
| No specific account number | Likely generic fraud |

When a scam is detected, you'll see a clear warning with the real agency's
number to verify before doing anything.

## Letter Templates Included

When writing is better than calling:

1. **Debt Validation Request** — stop collectors until they prove the debt is real
2. **Insurance Appeal Letter** — formal appeal with the right structure
3. **Response to Pay-or-Quit** — when you've already paid
4. **Request for Itemized Medical Bill** — your right, always
5. **Social Security Overpayment Waiver** — request forgiveness if you can't repay
6. **Jury Duty Hardship Excuse** — how to request postponement
